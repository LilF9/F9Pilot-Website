Introduction

Welcome to Frontier Airlines! This guide summarizes key sections of your Collective Bargaining Agreement (CBA) to help you get started. Inside, you'll find information on ALPA, CBA rules (especially for reserve pilots), essential websites & apps, and helpful tips.

ALPA

ALPA (Air Line Pilots Association) is the union for Frontier pilots and pilots at other airlines in North America. ALPA protects your rights, negotiates your contract, and offers career-long support. By uniting pilots, ALPA works to create a safer, fairer, and more professional workplace.

DART (Data Acquisition and Reporting Tool) is an ALPA system used to track pilot reports on safety, fatigue, and operational issues. New pilots are encouraged to use DART to report any issues they encounter, as it helps identify problems and advocate for pilots' interests.

CBA Explanations

Compensation & Pay (CBA Sections 3, 26, 4) üí∞

  • Monthly Guarantee: Monthly line holder is built within a Normal Credit Window of ~71 to ~85 hours, but this can be reduced by taking unpaid drops or leaves. Reserve is guaranteed a minimum of 75 hours of pay per month, or the hours they actually credit, whichever is greater. This pay can increase with additional pay credits earned from flying over eight hours on certain legs, working on a day off, or taking extra trips.
  • Overtime Pay: All Pay Credit over 82 hours a month is paid at 125%.
  • Pay Rigs: The CBA includes "Trip Rig" (pay for time away from base) and "Duty Rig" (minimum pay for certain trips) to ensure fair compensation.

Hours of Service & Scheduling (CBA Sections 12, 25) ‚úàÔ∏è

  • Duty Limits: A maximum of 14 hours of duty, with stricter limits for late-night flying.
  • Days Off: Guaranteed a minimum of 12 days off per month.
  • Rest Periods: Guaranteed a minimum of 10-11 hours of rest between duty periods and at least 31 consecutive hours of rest in any 7-day period.

Reserve - General Information (CBA Section 25.R)üìù

As a new pilot, you will likely spend significant time on Reserve. Short Call Reserves must be able to report to their domicile airport within two hours of a call from Crew Scheduling. You are required to be available to be contacted only during your assigned Reserve Duty Period (RDP). If you miss a call, you must return it within 10 minutes.

Note: While the CBA provides the framework, actual times and critical dates are determined by the monthly PBS bid.

Reserve Duty Period Times (Local Domicile Time):
  • RSV A: 0300 - 1330. Latest release is 1700.
  • 'B' RDP: 0800 - 1830. Latest release is 2200.
  • RSV C: 1200 - 2230. Latest release is 0200.
  • RSV D: 1700 - 2400. Latest release is 0700 (except 0900 for CDO's).
  • 'E' RDP: 1900 - 0200. Latest release is 0900 (except 1100 for CDO's).

Only the A, C, and D RDPs are biddable. The B and E RDPs are assigned by Crew Scheduling.

FDO - Fixed Day Off Reserve Lines üìÖ

Think of FDO as a long block of Long Call Reserve (LCR) days. You are not required to be on call at your domicile, but you must be scheduled for a 31-hour rest period within every 7-day period. Because of the predictable nature of FDO lines, they are not eligible for Aggressive Reserve or dropping/swapping days.

  • Call-out: The first day of your block is always Long Call. The company can convert your future days to Short Call with 12 hours' notice.
  • Release: On your last day of the block, you will be released by 1000 LDT if you don't have an assignment. If you are assigned a trip, it must be scheduled to end by 2359 LDT.

Want to Fly (WTF) List üìã

The "Want to Fly" list allows Reserve pilots to proactively seek out flying assignments. By calling Scheduling by 1200 LDT the day before, you can add your name to a list of pilots eager to be assigned. Available trips are then awarded to pilots on this list in seniority order.

Aggressive Reserve üöÄ

This is a way for you to actively bid for open trips. It's an opportunity to get a trip you want instead of waiting for Crew Scheduling to call you.

  • How it works: The company posts open trips on the Electronic Bid System by 1030 LDT the day before. You have until 1330 LDT to bid on them.
  • Awards: Trips are awarded by 1530 LDT, first to Short Call Reserves in seniority order, and then to Long Call Reserves in seniority order.
  • Commitment: Once you are awarded a trip, you cannot decline it. Think carefully before you bid.

Key Pilot Benefits & Rules

  • Sick Leave (CBA Section 14): Accrue one day per month, with a maximum bank of 120 days. You can "fly back" sick time to replenish your sick leave bank.
  • Vacations (CBA Section 7): Accrue vacation days annually based on your years of service.
  • Deadheading (CBA Section 8): You are guaranteed a Positive Space Must Ride seat and can deviate from your deadhead flight while still receiving pay credit.
  • Grievances (CBA Section 20): If you believe the company has violated the CBA, you have 60 days to file a grievance.
  • Retirement (CBA Sections 27, 28): The company makes non-elective contributions to your 401(k). These contributions are 100% vested after three years of service.

Helpful Tips üí°

  • Scheduling Mistakes: Don't be afraid to challenge crew scheduling if you believe they've made an error. Simply text your ALPA mentor or submit a DART for the situation.
  • Your Right to a Two-Hour Call-In: When on reserve, crew scheduling may sometimes ask you to report within the two-hour window. Remember, you have the contractual right to the full two hours to get to the airport. Don't hesitate to ask for it.
  • Utilize Your Union Rep: Your union reps are here to help you. Never think you're "bugging" them by sending a DART or a text. They are glad to assist and there are multiple people on each committee, so if one is busy, another will happily respond.
  • Filing a Grievance: If you believe the company has broken a contract rule, do not be afraid to file a grievance report. This is a formal way to hold the company accountable for its actions and protect your rights. If this is your first time, I would highly recommend submitting a DART to the Grievance Committee first for their assistance.
  • Speak Up, Even on Probation: Your probationary period is a time of evaluation, but it doesn't mean you should stay silent on safety or contractual issues. When something seems wrong, contact your union first so they can guide you. The system works because pilots, including new hires, use it to report issues and advocate for a safer workplace.
  • Stay Informed: When you have downtime, consider glancing over sections of the CBA that are relevant to your current situation, such as Section 25.R (Reserve), to become more familiar with the rules and your rights.

Important Websites & Apps

Familiarize yourself with these systems for daily operations:

  • ELP: The ELP app is used for viewing your schedule, flight assignments, and accepting pairing changes. The ELP Crew Portal is used for looking at your schedule and accepting assignments. Both are available to download on iOS & Android.
  • ELP CrewPay: The ELP CrewPay portal is used for checking sick time and pay reports.
  • Navblue: This is the system used for flight planning, monthly bidding, and managing your schedule. For step-by-step guidance, check out the PBS Bidding Guide and watch the PBS training videos. You can also listen to the Five by Five podcast episode ‚ÄúSet Condition | Unstacking Your PBS Bid‚Äù, which covers common mistakes and tips to get the most out of PBS. If you still need assistance, you can always submit a DART to Scheduling for support.
  • FLICA: You can trade, drop, or pick up open time flights, as well as bid for premium trips, FDO, Pilot Domicile Trade, and vacation. It is also used for the pilot system bid for changing bases.

Probationary Period

Your probationary period begins on your date of hire and lasts for 12 months or until you successfully complete your Probationary Proficiency Check, whichever is later. The probationary period can be extended by leaves of absence of 30 days or more. While on probation, you have many of the same rights and privileges as non-probationary pilots.
